## What is Continuous Testing? 

Continuous testing is an automated, real-time QA approach embedded in the software development lifecycle (SDLC). It offers benefits such as faster feedback, cost-efficiency, and higher-quality user experiences by detecting defects early. With key features like automation, CI/CD integration, and real-time insights, continuous testing has become essential for agile development.

As organizations demand faster deployments, continuous testing becomes a critical enabler. It seamlessly integrates testing into every stage of development, allowing companies to identify and fix critical bugs early in the pipeline.

AI introduces revolutionary potential to QA by automating test creation, prioritizing high-risk areas, and dynamically adapting to evolving workflows. Some notable ways AI impacts continuous testing include:

- **Automated Test Creation**: Using AI to generate test scripts, reducing manual workload.
- **Risk-Based Testing**: Focusing on high-priority areas that may cause system failures.
- **Adapting with Agility**: Dynamically adjusting test cases to keep up with ever-changing code.
