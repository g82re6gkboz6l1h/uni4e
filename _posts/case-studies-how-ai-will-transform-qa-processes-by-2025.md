## Current Roadblocks in QA and Their Implications

QA processes confront substantial hurdles, from manual testing inefficiencies to automated testing limitations. These challenges vastly impact operational costs, testing scalability, and the accuracy in identifying bugs.

### **1. Issues With Manual Testing**
Delivering precision takes time and effort, but manual testing reveals its flaws in key areas:
- **Tedious Execution:** Lengthy testing slows down product launches.
- **Error-Prone:** Human fatigue and complexity lead to missed defects.
- **Poor Scalability:** Large-scale and intricate systems overwhelm manual testers.
- **High Costs:** Staffing and resources elevate testing expenses.

### **2. Automated Testing Shortcomings**
Automation introduced speed but carries inefficiencies:
- **Static Testing Scripts:** Scripts require updates to remain relevant.
- **Adaptability Challenges:** Tools fail to sync with changing environments.
- **Overhead Costs:** Maintenance and training investments mount.
- **False Positives/Negatives:** Imperfect bug detection adds debug cycles.

AI has emerged as the game-changer poised to attack these limitations head-on via platforms like [Zof AI](https://zof.ai).
