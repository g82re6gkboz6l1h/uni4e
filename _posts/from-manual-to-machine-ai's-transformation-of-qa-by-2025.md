## A Journey Through QA Testing Evolution

### Manual Testing and Its Limitations
In the early days of software development, QA relied heavily on manual testing methods, which were labor-intensive and prone to human error. QA engineers would run repetitive trials and document checklists to identify bugs—a slow and restrictive process.

### Automating QA Endeavors
The introduction of automated testing tools like Selenium transformed QA in the 2000s. Repetitive tasks such as regression testing became streamlined, but scalability remained a challenge and continued reliance on human intervention slowed down delivery timelines.

### Continuous Testing Revolution
With Agile and DevOps methodologies popularized in the 2010s, QA became part of a continuous cycle. Testing automation integrated into CI/CD pipelines boosted delivery speeds, yet complexities in growing codebases exposed scalability challenges.
