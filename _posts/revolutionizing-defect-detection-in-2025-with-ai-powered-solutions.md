## Addressing Existing Challenges in QA Defect Detection

Despite advancements in technology, QA processes are still hindered by significant obstacles, including:

### 1. **Mistakes from Human Oversight**
Human error remains a prominent issue, particularly in software testing or manual product inspections in manufacturing. Fatigue, distractions, or cognitive overload can result in missed flaws or inconsistencies.

### 2. **Misidentified Defects (False Positives/Negatives)**
Traditional QA approaches are prone to inaccuracies. False positives waste resources by flagging non-issues, whereas false negatives can result in serious defects going unnoticed, harming quality and reputation.

### 3. **Speed vs. Accuracy Trade-Offs**
As industries focus on faster production cycles or continuous software integration, conventional defect detection methods often fail to keep pace.

### 4. **Data Overload**
With the rise of IoT-powered systems and digital twins, QA teams now face an abundance of data. Extracting actionable insights—or pinpointing defects in real time—overwhelms legacy systems.

### 5. **Adapting to New Defect Patterns**
Evolving technologies introduce novel defect patterns that traditional systems cannot analyze efficiently, leading to poor adaptation rates.
