## What is Autonomous Testing? Benefits Explained

Autonomous testing leverages AI to design, execute, and assess software tests without human intervention. Unlike traditional automated testing, it uses machine learning to adapt and evolve. This reduces human workload, increases accuracy, speeds up testing cycles, and enhances cost efficiency, thereby empowering QA engineers to focus on strategic oversight. Challenges in AI-Driven QA

Despite its potential, autonomous testing faces:
- High initial investment costs.
- Learning curves for QA personnel.
- Ethical concerns over job displacement.
- False positives in defect detection.
- Data privacy challenges during real-world tests.

Addressing these will be crucial to fully unlocking its capabilities.
